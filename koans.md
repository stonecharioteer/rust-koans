## 🦀 Rust Koans (Git-style)

### 1. Ownership

> The novice said:
> “I assigned `s` to `t`, and now `s` is gone.”
>
> The master said:
> “Yes.”
>
> The novice waited.
>
> The master said:
> “That is all.”

---

### 2. Move semantics

> The novice said:
> “But I only wanted a copy.”
>
> The master replied:
> “Then you should have asked for one.”

---

### 3. Borrowing

> The novice said:
> “I borrowed a value, and Rust will not let me change it.”
>
> The master said:
> “Correct.”
>
> The novice said:
> “But I need to change it.”
>
> The master said:
> “Then do not borrow it.”

---

### 4. Mutable references

> The novice said:
> “I created two mutable references.”
>
> The master said:
> “No.”
>
> The novice checked the compiler.
>
> The master said nothing.

---

### 5. Lifetimes

> The novice said:
> “I added lifetimes, and it compiles.”
>
> The master said:
> “Do you understand them?”
>
> The novice said:
> “No.”
>
> The master said:
> “Then nothing has changed.”

---

### 6. Clone

> The novice said:
> “I fixed the error by cloning.”
>
> The master said:
> “Yes.”
>
> The novice said:
> “Is this good?”
>
> The master said:
> “It compiles.”

---

### 7. Compiler

> The novice said:
> “The compiler is too strict.”
>
> The master said:
> “And yet, it is not strict enough to write your program for you.”

---

### 8. Unsafe

> The novice said:
> “I used `unsafe` and now it works.”
>
> The master said:
> “It always works… until it doesn’t.”

---

### 9. Iterators

> The novice said:
> “I don’t understand iterators.”
>
> The master said:
> “Then write a loop.”
>
> The novice did.
>
> The master said:
> “Now you understand why iterators exist.”

---

### 10. Error handling

> The novice said:
> “I used `unwrap()` everywhere.”
>
> The master said:
> “Then you have decided where your program will panic.”
>
> The novice said:
> “Yes.”
>
> The master said:
> “Not where.”
